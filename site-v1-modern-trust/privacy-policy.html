Client Privacy Notice · Current & Former Clients

Privacy Policy

How KFN Accounting & Financial, LLC collects, uses, and protects the personal and financial information you entrust to us — stated plainly, on the record.

In Plain Terms

We use your information to prepare your returns and serve your accounts — nothing more. We don't sell it, and we don't share it except as the law permits.

The Notice

We value your privacy and protect your personal data.

KFN Accounting & Financial, LLC · Applies to current and former clients

In the course of preparing your tax returns and managing your accounts, you have provided us with certain personal and financial information necessary to process your transactions. This notice explains what we collect, how it is used and disclosed, and how it is protected.

I.The information we collect

We collect nonpublic personal information about you from the following sources:

  • InterviewsInformation we receive from you in interviews regarding your tax situation.
  • Applications & formsInformation you provide on applications, organizers, and other forms — such as your name, address, telephone number, Social Security number, dependents, and income.
  • Tax documentsRecords you supply for preparation, including Forms W-2, 1099, and other tax-related documents.

II.How your information is used and disclosed

“We will not disclose any nonpublic personal information about our customers or former customers to anyone, except as permitted by law.”

Where disclosure is necessary, it is limited to the service providers we engage to help process your transactions or maintain your accounts — and only to the extent needed to perform those services. We do not sell or distribute your information to third parties.

III.How we protect your information

We restrict access to your personal and financial information to those employees who need it to provide services to you. We maintain physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards that comply with federal standards to guard your nonpublic personal information.

IV.What you need to do

Nothing — no action is required on your part. This notice is provided for your reference, and you may wish to file it with your other financial records.
