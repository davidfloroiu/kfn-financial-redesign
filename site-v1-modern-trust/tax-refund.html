Where's your refund?

Every refund tracker worth using, in one place — the official federal and Illinois tools, current processing windows, and the three details to have in hand before you check.

Go straight to the trackers
In Plain Terms

A refund is money you already earned — you paid in more than you owed, and the government is returning the difference. These official tools show exactly where it is.

The Official Trackers

Three tools cover every return.

Federal, Illinois, and amended returns are each tracked in a different place. All three tools are free, official, and open around the clock.

01

IRS “Where's My Refund?”

The federal tracker. Your status appears within about 24 hours of e-filing — or roughly four weeks after mailing a paper return — and moves through three stages: Return Received, Refund Approved, Refund Sent.

irs.gov — checks your federal refund 02

MyTax Illinois

The State of Illinois tracker, run by the Illinois Department of Revenue. Check the status of your IL-1040 refund directly — no account is required for a simple status inquiry.

mytax.illinois.gov — checks your Illinois refund 03

IRS “Where's My Amended Return?”

Amended returns (Form 1040-X) never appear in the standard tracker. This dedicated tool follows them through Received, Adjusted, and Completed — for the current year and up to three prior years.

irs.gov — checks amended returns only

Filed outside Illinois? Every state with an income tax runs its own refund tracker. KFN serves clients in all 50 states — email contact@kfnfinancial.com and we'll point you to the right one.

Before You Check

Have these three things ready.

Every tracker asks for the same information. With your return in front of you, the check takes less than a minute.

  • Social Security number or ITINThe primary taxpayer's number, exactly as it appears on the return.
  • Filing statusSingle, married filing jointly, married filing separately, head of household, or qualifying surviving spouse.
  • Exact refund amountThe whole-dollar figure from your return. Close doesn't count — the tools match to the dollar.

On your phone instead?

The IRS's official mobile app, IRS2Go, checks your federal refund status with the same three details — no desktop required.

However you check, the answer only changes once a day: the IRS updates statuses overnight. A daily look is all it takes — refreshing hourly surfaces nothing new.

Also available IRS2Go — iOS & Android
Current Timing

How long refunds actually take.

Much of what you'll read online is out of date. These are the IRS's current published windows, assuming a complete and accurate return.

01

E-filed with direct deposit

The fastest route by a wide margin. The IRS issues most refunds in under three weeks, and direct deposit skips the mail entirely.

Most within 21 days
02

Paper return

Paper returns are processed by hand, so the clock runs longer — allow at least a month before the tracker shows meaningful movement.

4 weeks or more
03

Amended return (Form 1040-X)

Amended returns take the longest. Status appears in the dedicated amended-return tracker about three weeks after filing, and processing can run considerably longer.

Up to 16 weeks

These windows are IRS guidance, not guarantees. Returns claiming certain credits, filed near a deadline, or selected for additional review can take longer — the trackers will tell you if the IRS needs anything from you.
