When to Hire

Three signs it's time for financial leadership.

01

You've outgrown bookkeeping

The books are current and the filings are on time — but no one is telling you what the numbers mean for next quarter's decisions.

In plain English: you need answers, not just records.
02

You're raising capital

Lenders and investors expect forecasts, models, and financial statements that hold up to scrutiny — built and defended by someone who has done it before.

In plain English: numbers that survive due diligence.
03

You're planning an exit

A strong sale is won long before the closing table — clean financials, efficient systems, and a business that can run without you.

In plain English: get sale-ready before you need to be.
